About Us

Orchardists rely on crop counting and vine data to predict fruit yield, promote growth and protect plants, but with manual methods, this data collection is time consuming, inaccurate and requires oversight and administration.

Acuris Systems is an automation service that will allow it's customers to leave that in the past, using robotics and artificial intelligence to collect this data faster, safer and more accurately than manual methods. Additionally, we present this data in an intuitive package that lets our customers know their crop without any spreadsheet hassle.

We take your 1% sample size with 75% accuracy and transform that into 100% sample size with over 95% accuracy.

Beyond developing a solution for crop counting and vine-mapping, we aim to analyse the quality of fruit, detect disease and eventually automate other growing processes, such as spraying pesticide and picking fruit.

How It Works

Acuris plans on scaling up our fully-functional prototype to a fleet of robots and beginning large scale testing next year.
We intend on rolling out as a hardware service with no upfront investment in our robots, but rather paying as you use. Acuris technicians will be on-site for less than a day, overseeing a fleet of robots which will photograph and track a comprehensive data map of the orchard, before providing our customer with this information in a matter of days.

Our robots run at the pace of a brisk jog, each scanning a 100 metre row, autonomously, in less than a minute. Data is then offloaded to out artificial intelligence, which uses photographs aligned with geo-spatial information to accurately map every feature on the orchard, including bay-poles, vines, fruit, fruitlet, buds and flowers, down to the millimetre.

Team

Acuris Systems was founded in 2017, Matthew Warner, founder and lead engineer, developed the first two prototypes while working out of his living room. After participating in the Sprout agri-tech accelerator and winning an innovation award during the 2018 field days. Now, we are a team of five. Our fifth version of the robot and our supporting software are both complete and in active testing.
